About spotting in perimenopause: Spotting between periods is very common as oestrogen levels fluctuate. It's usually harmless but always worth mentioning to your GP if it happens frequently or after sex.
Why do symptoms cluster? Fluctuating oestrogen affects the brain, bones, skin and more — which is why perimenopause can feel like so many different things at once. Tracking them helps identify your personal patterns.
What are triggers? Certain lifestyle factors can worsen hot flashes, sleep and mood. Logging them alongside symptoms helps reveal personal patterns — e.g. alcohol and hot flash frequency.
Your personal tags: Add anything that matters to your health picture but isn't in the standard lists — e.g. "migraine", "gym", "work stress", "therapy". Manage your tag library in Settings.
Mood & perimenopause: Low oestrogen directly affects serotonin, which regulates mood. Anxiety, irritability and tearfulness are neurological symptoms — not a sign of weakness. You can select multiple moods.

❤️ Apple Health
ⓘ How Apple Health sync works in Luna
Luna runs entirely in your browser as a local HTML file. Apple's HealthKit API is only available to native iOS apps installed through the App Store — it cannot be accessed from a website or browser-based app, by Apple's design.

What Luna can do: You can export your Luna data as a JSON backup and a future native app version could read from HealthKit directly. Until then, the best bridge is manual CSV import from any health tracker that exports data.
In the meantime, you can import health data exported from Apple Health or wearables (Oura, Garmin, Fitbit) using the CSV import below. Luna will read resting heart rate, sleep duration and body temperature columns if present and merge them into your daily logs.
How to export from Apple Health: 1. Open the Health app → tap your profile photo
2. Tap Export All Health Data → Share → Save to Files
3. This creates a ZIP containing XML files — use a third-party app like Health Export CSV (App Store, free tier available) to convert to CSV
4. Import the resulting CSV here

From Oura / Garmin / Fitbit: Log in to their web dashboard → Account → Export Data → choose CSV → import here.

Columns Luna looks for: date, resting_heart_rate, sleep_duration_hours, temperature_deviation (or skin_temp_deviation)
🔒 Privacy & Data
Your data never leaves your device. Luna stores everything in your browser’s local storage — it is not uploaded, shared, or transmitted anywhere. No account is required. No third parties can access your health data.

Use Export Backup to download all your data as a JSON file. Use Import Backup to restore it on a new device or after clearing your browser.

⚠ Danger zone — this cannot be undone. Export a backup first if you want to keep your data.
